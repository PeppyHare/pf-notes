    Rules of thumb

    Jarboe's 'Rules of Thumb' - basic associations and concepts to keep in mind when working with fusion plasmas

    Rule 1

    In event counting measurements the uncertainty in event rate is equal to the square root of the number of events detected. A trade off is made between signal resolution and time resolution

    Rule 2

    The total E and M force on a volume of plasma can be computed from surface values through F=∫T‾⋅ndV \vec F = \int \overline{T} \cdot \vec n \dd V F=∫T⋅ndV

    Rule 3

    Magnetic field has a tension and isotropic pressure

    T=B2μ0p=B22μ0 T = \frac{B^2}{\mu_0} \qquad p = \frac{B^2}{2 \mu_0} T=μ0​B2​p=2μ0​B2​

    Rule 4

    Plasma is frozen in the magnetic field when η=0 \eta = 0 η=0 (zero resistivity).

    Rule 5

    When vmag \vec{v}_{mag} vmag​ is the velocity of the magnetic field,

    E=−vmag×B \vec E = - \vec{v}_{mag} \cross \vec B E=−vmag​×B

    Rule 6

    Ambipolar diffusion requires the ions and electrons to leave the plasma at the same rate

    newvew=niwviw n_{ew} v_{ew} = n_{iw} v_{iw} new​vew​=niw​viw​

    Rule 7

    At a given temperature the electron speed is 60 times the D ions requiring a four-e-folding in sheath voltage to make the electron and ion losses equal

    Vsheath≈4Te V_{sheath} \approx 4 T_e Vsheath​≈4Te​

    Rule 9

    A Langmuir probe can measure Te T_e Te​ Ti T_i Ti​ and n n n.     Rule 10

    Particles interact within a Debye sphere. Outside λD \lambda_D λD​ distance particles do not see each other due to shielding.

    Rule 11: Four collision frequencies

    • νei \nu_{ei} νei​ - Electron momentum loss rate on ions. Used in resistivity.
    • νee \nu_{ee} νee​ Electron energy exchange rate with electrons. In other words, if you do something to the electrons this is how long it will take to get back to Maxwellian. Same order as νei \nu_{ei} νei​
    • νii \nu_{ii} νii​ Ion energy exchange rate with ions
    • νie \nu_{ie} νie​ Electron energy exchange rate between electrons and ions. It's about the same as ions slowing down in electrons: ≈memiνee \approx \frac{m_e}{m_i} \nu_{ee} ≈mi​me​​νee​. For fusion to work, need confinement times longer than this time.
