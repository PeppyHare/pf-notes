    10.1.1 Maxwell Stress Tensor

    The Maxwell stress tensor encompasses the way that electromagnetic fields an exert forces/stresses in 3D space:

    p= momentum  \vec p = \text{ momentum } p= momentum 

    The basic force laws of E&M are

    d pd t=q(E+v×B) \dv{\vec p}{t} = q \left( \vec E + \vec v \cross \vec B \right) dtdp​=q(E+v×B)

    On an element of volume, the change in momentum is

    d Pd t=∫V(ρE+j×B)dV \dv{P}{t} = \int_V (\rho E + j \cross B) \dd V dtdP​=∫V​(ρE+j×B)dV

    Using the chain rule ∂∂t(E×B)=∂E∂t×B+E×∂B∂t \pdv{}{t} (E \cross B) = \pdv{E}{t} \cross B + E \cross \pdv{B}{t} ∂t∂​(E×B)=∂t∂E​×B+E×∂t∂B​ and B(∇⋅B)=0 B( \div B) = 0 B(∇⋅B)=0
