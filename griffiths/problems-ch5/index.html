    Problem 5.5

    A current I flows down a wire of radius a. (a) If it is uniformly distributed over the surface, what is the surface current density K? (b) If it is distributed in such a way that the volume current density is inversely proportional to the distance from the axis, what is J(s)?

    K=I2πa K = \frac{I}{2 \pi a} K=2πaI​

    Suppose instead the current is distributed somehow throughout the volume of the wire such that the current density is inversely proportional to the distance from the axis. Then

    j=currentunit area ⊥ flow=dIda⊥ j = \frac{\text{current}}{\text{unit area } \perp \text{ flow}} = \frac{d I}{da_{\perp}} j=unit area ⊥ flowcurrent​=da⊥​dI​

    We suppose that j has the form

    j=const.s=cs j = \frac{\text{const.}}{s} = \frac{c}{s} j=sconst.​=sc​

    I=∫jda⊥=∫0a∫02πcssdsdϕ=2πca \begin{aligned} I & = \int j \dd a_{\perp} \\ & = \int_{0} ^a \int_{0} ^{2 \pi} \frac{c}{s} s \dd s \dd \phi \\ & = 2 \pi c a \end{aligned} I​=∫jda⊥​=∫0a​∫02π​sc​sdsdϕ=2πca​

    so

    c=I2πa c = \frac{I}{2 \pi a} c=2πaI​

    and

    j=I2πas j = \frac{I}{2 \pi a s} j=2πasI​

    Problem 5.37

    (a) A phonograph record of radius R, carrying a uniform surface charge sigma sigma sigma is rotating at constant angular velocity ω \omega ω. Find its magnetic dipole moment. (b) Find the magnetic dipole moment of the spinning spherical shell in Example 5.11. Show that for points r>R r > R r>R the potential is that of a perfect dipole.

    (a) We get the monopole moment by integrating over the disk of the record. For a ring at radius r, m=Iπr2 m = I \pi r^2 m=Iπr2. In this case,

    I→σvdr=σωrdr I \rightarrow \sigma v \dd r = \sigma \omega r \dd r I→σvdr=σωrdr

    m=∫0Rπr2σωrdr=πσωR4/4 m = \int _0 ^R \pi r^2 \sigma \omega r \dd r = \pi \sigma \omega R^4 / 4 m=∫0R​πr2σωrdr=πσωR4/4

    (b) To get the magnetic dipole moment of our sphere, we need to integrate over the surface of the sphere:

    The total charge on the shaded ring is dq=σ(2πRsin⁡θ)Rdθ \dd q = \sigma (2 \pi R \sin \theta) R \dd \theta dq=σ(2πRsinθ)Rdθ. The time to make one revolution is dt=2πω \dd t = 2 \pi \omega dt=2πω, so the current in the ring is

    I=dqdt=σωR2sin⁡θdθ I = \frac{dq}{dt} = \sigma \omega R^2 \sin \theta \dd \theta I=dtdq​=σωR2sinθdθ

    The area of the ring is π(Rsin⁡θ)2 \pi (R \sin \theta)^2 π(Rsinθ)2, so the magnetic moment of the ring is

    dm=(σωR2sin⁡θdθ)πR2sin⁡2θ \dd m = (\sigma \omega R^2 \sin \theta \dd \theta) \pi R^2 \sin ^2 \theta dm=(σωR2sinθdθ)πR2sin2θ

    m=σωπR4∫0πsin⁡3θdθ=(4/3)σωπR4 m = \sigma \omega \pi R^4 \int_0 ^\pi \sin ^3 \theta \dd \theta = (4 / 3) \sigma \omega \pi R^4 m=σωπR4∫0π​sin3θdθ=(4/3)σωπR4

    and we know that m points in the z^ \vu{z} z^ direction (right-hand-rule), so

    m=4π3σωR4z^ \vec{m} = \frac{4 \pi}{3} \sigma \omega R^4 \vu{z} m=34π​σωR4z^
